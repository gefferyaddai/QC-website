Modeling of option prices September 2025

Black-Scholes Framework

A foundational mathematical model used to price options by estimating their fair value based on factors such as the underlying asset price, volatility, time to expiration, risk-free rate, and strike price. It assumes continuous market conditions and provides a closed-form solution for European-style options, making it a cornerstone of modern derivatives pricing.

Probablistic Modeling November 2025

Simulating Asset returns using Monte Carlo methods

A computational technique that uses repeated random sampling to model uncertainty and simulate a wide range of possible outcomes. In finance, it is commonly used to forecast asset price paths, value complex derivatives, and assess risk by generating thousands of potential scenarios based on probabilistic inputs.

portfolio optimization Februrary 2026

Black-Litterman model application

An advanced portfolio optimization framework that combines market equilibrium returns with investor views using a Bayesian approach. It improves upon traditional mean-variance optimization by producing more stable and intuitive asset allocations, allowing investors to systematically incorporate subjective insights into portfolio construction.
